LIQUIDITY RISK MANAGEMENT POLICY
Document ID: IPS-004
Effective Date: 2024-04-01
Last Review: 2025-06-01
Owner: Risk Manager

1. PURPOSE

This policy establishes the framework for managing liquidity risk across the Medical Grants Investment Portfolio, ensuring the organization can meet grant disbursement obligations and capital call commitments at all times.

2. LIQUIDITY RISK FRAMEWORK

2.1 Definition: Liquidity risk is the risk that the portfolio cannot meet cash obligations (grant disbursements, capital calls, operating expenses) without incurring unacceptable losses.

2.2 Key Metrics:
- Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR): Liquid assets / 12-month projected outflows >= 1.2x
- Illiquidity Ratio: Locked/annual-redemption assets / total portfolio <= 35%
- Capital Call Coverage: Available cash + near-term distributions / 12-month projected capital calls >= 1.1x

3. LIQUIDITY CLASSIFICATION

3.1 Highly Liquid (redeemable within 30 days):
- Cash and cash equivalents
- Money market funds
- Public equity funds with daily/weekly liquidity
- Expected: 20-25% of portfolio

3.2 Moderately Liquid (redeemable within 90 days):
- Hedge funds with monthly redemption
- Credit funds with quarterly redemption
- Expected: 25-35% of portfolio

3.3 Semi-Liquid (redeemable within 1 year):
- Funds with annual redemption terms
- Expected: 15-25% of portfolio

3.4 Illiquid (locked or >1 year):
- Venture capital funds
- Private equity co-investments
- Locked hedge funds
- Expected: maximum 35% of portfolio

4. MONITORING AND REPORTING

4.1 Weekly:
- Cash position and near-term obligations
- Capital call notices received
- Redemption requests in process

4.2 Monthly:
- Full liquidity profile by classification tier
- LCR calculation and trend
- Capital call forecast update
- Distribution pipeline

4.3 Quarterly:
- Comprehensive liquidity stress test
- Fund-level liquidity term review
- Grant disbursement forecast vs available liquidity
- Illiquidity budget utilization

5. STRESS TESTING

5.1 Scenarios:
- Base case: Normal operations, expected cash flows
- Stress case 1: 20% of expected distributions delayed by 6 months
- Stress case 2: Unexpected capital calls of 150% of forecast
- Stress case 3: Market downturn reducing liquid asset values by 25%
- Tail risk: Combination of scenarios 1-3

5.2 Actions by Stress Level:
- Green (LCR > 1.5x): Normal operations
- Yellow (LCR 1.2-1.5x): Increase monitoring frequency, defer new illiquid commitments
- Red (LCR < 1.2x): Activate liquidity action plan, consider redemptions from liquid funds

6. LIQUIDITY ACTION PLAN

If liquidity metrics breach thresholds:

Step 1: Immediate assessment of cash position and upcoming obligations
Step 2: Identify available redemption capacity across liquid funds
Step 3: Submit redemption notices for overweight liquid positions
Step 4: Defer new capital commitments pending resolution
Step 5: Communicate with Investment Committee within 24 hours
Step 6: If necessary, negotiate with fund managers for early redemption or secondary sale

7. FUND LIQUIDITY TERMS REVIEW

Before any new investment, assess:
- Redemption frequency and notice period
- Lock-up period and any extensions
- Gate provisions (fund-level and investor-level)
- Side pocket risk
- Secondary market liquidity for the fund type
- Historical redemption processing experience

8. GOVERNANCE

8.1 Risk Manager: Primary responsibility for liquidity monitoring and reporting
8.2 CIO: Approves liquidity action plan activation
8.3 Investment Committee: Reviews quarterly liquidity reports, approves policy changes
