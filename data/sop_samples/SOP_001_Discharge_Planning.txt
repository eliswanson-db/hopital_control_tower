MEMORIAL HEALTH SYSTEM -- STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
Document: SOP-001-DP
Title: Patient Discharge Planning and Length of Stay Management
Effective Date: 2024-01-15
Review Date: 2025-01-15
Department: All Clinical Departments
Approved By: Chief Medical Officer, VP of Operations

1. PURPOSE

This procedure establishes standardized discharge planning protocols to reduce
avoidable extended lengths of stay, minimize readmissions, and ensure safe transitions
of care. Target average LOS: under 5.0 days system-wide.

2. SCOPE

Applies to all inpatient encounters across Memorial Health System hospitals
(Hospital A, Hospital B, Hospital C). Covers admission through discharge and 30-day
post-discharge follow-up.

3. DISCHARGE PLANNING INITIATION

3.1 Discharge planning begins within 24 hours of admission.
3.2 The attending physician documents an estimated discharge date (EDD) in the EHR.
3.3 Care coordinators review all admissions daily by 10:00 AM for discharge readiness.
3.4 Patients with LOS > 3 days trigger an automatic escalation to the department
    discharge planning lead.

4. MONDAY DISCHARGE PROTOCOL

4.1 Background: Analysis shows patients discharged on Mondays have 15-20% higher LOS
    than other weekdays due to weekend care gaps and delayed weekend rounding.

4.2 Mitigation Steps:
    a) Weekend rounding teams must complete discharge assessments by Saturday 2:00 PM
       for all patients with EDD of Sunday or Monday.
    b) Pharmacy must prepare discharge medications for Monday discharges by Sunday
       5:00 PM.
    c) Transportation and home health referrals for Monday discharges must be
       confirmed by Friday 3:00 PM.
    d) Department heads review Monday discharge volumes each Friday and staff
       accordingly.

4.3 Performance Target: Reduce Monday LOS gap to < 5% above weekday average within
    6 months of implementation.

5. LOS ESCALATION THRESHOLDS

5.1 LOS > 5 days: Automatic notification to department medical director.
5.2 LOS > 7 days: Case review by utilization management committee within 24 hours.
5.3 LOS > 10 days: Executive escalation to VP of Operations with barrier analysis.
5.4 For each escalation tier, the reviewing authority must document:
    - Primary barrier to discharge
    - Action plan with owner and target date
    - Whether the stay is clinically appropriate

6. READMISSION PREVENTION

6.1 All patients with diagnoses associated with > 15% readmission risk receive:
    - Post-discharge phone call within 48 hours
    - Follow-up appointment scheduled before discharge
    - Medication reconciliation completed and reviewed with patient
6.2 Patients readmitted within 30 days trigger a root-cause analysis led by the
    department quality lead.
6.3 Readmission rate target: < 10% system-wide. Departments exceeding 12% for two
    consecutive months enter a performance improvement plan.

7. DEPARTMENT-SPECIFIC GUIDELINES

7.1 Cardiology: Average LOS target 4.5 days. Heart failure patients require
    standardized discharge checklist (weight monitoring plan, medication list,
    dietary counseling, follow-up within 7 days).
7.2 Orthopedics: Average LOS target 3.0 days for elective joint replacement.
    Same-day physical therapy consult required.
7.3 General Surgery: Average LOS target 3.5 days. Enhanced recovery protocol
    (ERAS) compliance tracked monthly.

8. REPORTING AND COMPLIANCE

8.1 Department heads receive weekly LOS dashboards by hospital and department.
8.2 Monthly LOS review at Operations Committee with trend analysis.
8.3 Quarterly readmission root-cause analysis report to CMO.
8.4 Annual review of this SOP by Quality Improvement Committee.

9. REFERENCES

- CMS Conditions of Participation, Discharge Planning (42 CFR 482.43)
- Joint Commission Standard PC.04.01.01 (Discharge Planning)
- Institute for Healthcare Improvement: Reducing Avoidable Readmissions
