INVESTMENT COMPLIANCE PROCEDURES
Document ID: IPS-005
Effective Date: 2024-05-01
Last Review: 2025-06-01
Owner: Compliance Officer

1. PURPOSE

This document defines the compliance monitoring procedures for the Medical Grants Investment Portfolio, ensuring adherence to the Investment Policy Statement, regulatory requirements, and fiduciary obligations.

2. COMPLIANCE MONITORING FRAMEWORK

2.1 Pre-Trade Compliance:
- All new investments screened against IPS guidelines before execution
- Concentration limits checked prior to allocation
- Liquidity impact assessed for each new commitment
- Sanctions and restricted list screening for fund managers

2.2 Post-Trade Compliance:
- Daily: Position limits and concentration checks
- Weekly: Cash and liquidity ratio monitoring
- Monthly: Full IPS guideline compliance review
- Quarterly: Comprehensive compliance report to Investment Committee

3. KEY COMPLIANCE METRICS

3.1 Portfolio-Level:
- Asset allocation within approved ranges (per IPS-001 Section 3)
- Single fund concentration <= 10% of portfolio
- Single manager concentration <= 15% of portfolio
- Top-5 strategy concentration <= 40%
- Illiquidity ratio <= 35%
- Liquidity Coverage Ratio >= 1.2x

3.2 Fund-Level:
- All funds have current audited financials (within 180 days)
- All funds registered with appropriate regulators
- Annual operational due diligence review completed
- Side letter terms documented and monitored
- Key-person provisions tracked

4. BREACH PROCEDURES

4.1 Soft Breach (within tolerance bands):
- Document in compliance log
- Notify CIO within 2 business days
- Develop remediation plan with timeline
- Monitor weekly until resolved

4.2 Hard Breach (outside tolerance):
- Immediate notification to CIO and Investment Committee Chair
- Trading restrictions on affected asset class until resolved
- Formal remediation plan required within 5 business days
- Report to Board at next meeting

4.3 Regulatory Breach:
- Immediate escalation to General Counsel and Compliance Officer
- Regulatory notification as required
- Root cause analysis within 10 business days
- Policy amendment if systemic issue identified

5. WATCHLIST MANAGEMENT

5.1 Watchlist Placement Criteria:
- Underperformance: Returns below benchmark by >300bps for 2 consecutive quarters
- Key-person event: Departure of named key person
- Regulatory/legal: Any enforcement action, investigation, or material litigation
- Operational: Failed audit, administrator change, or material control weakness
- AUM decline: >20% AUM decline from peak (potential for adverse selection)
- Style drift: Material deviation from stated investment strategy

5.2 Watchlist Monitoring:
- Monthly performance review with enhanced attribution
- Quarterly call with fund management
- Review of all investor communications
- Assessment of redemption queue and gate risk

5.3 Watchlist Resolution:
- Upgrade to active: Issue resolved, 2 consecutive quarters of acceptable performance
- Maintain on watchlist: Issue ongoing but contained
- Initiate redemption: Issue worsening or unresolvable, per IPS-001 Section 6.3

6. REGULATORY COMPLIANCE

6.1 Fiduciary Standards:
- All investment decisions documented with fiduciary rationale
- Conflicts of interest disclosed and managed
- Best execution policy for all transactions
- Fee and expense transparency for all fund investments

6.2 Tax Compliance:
- K-1 tracking for US partnership investments
- FATCA/CRS compliance for offshore funds
- UBTI monitoring for tax-exempt entities
- Withholding tax optimization review annually

6.3 ESG Compliance:
- Annual ESG screening of all fund investments
- Controversial weapons and sanctions screening
- Climate risk disclosure tracking
- UN PRI reporting alignment

7. REPORTING

7.1 Monthly Compliance Dashboard:
- Allocation vs targets with deviation flags
- Concentration metrics (fund, manager, strategy, sector)
- Liquidity ratios and trends
- Watchlist status summary

7.2 Quarterly Compliance Report:
- Full IPS guideline compliance assessment
- All breaches and remediation status
- Watchlist additions, removals, and status changes
- Regulatory and fiduciary compliance attestation
- Recommendations for policy amendments

8. RECORD RETENTION

- Investment memos and approvals: 10 years
- Compliance reports and breach logs: 7 years
- Due diligence files: Life of investment + 5 years
- Meeting minutes and decisions: 10 years
- Communications with fund managers: 5 years
