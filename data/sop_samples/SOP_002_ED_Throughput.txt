MEMORIAL HEALTH SYSTEM -- STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
Document: SOP-002-ED
Title: Emergency Department Throughput and Wait Time Management
Effective Date: 2024-03-01
Review Date: 2025-03-01
Department: Emergency Medicine
Approved By: ED Medical Director, Chief Nursing Officer

1. PURPOSE

This procedure establishes protocols for managing Emergency Department throughput,
wait time thresholds, and escalation procedures to ensure timely patient care and
reduce left-without-being-seen (LWBS) rates.

2. SCOPE

Applies to all ED encounters at Memorial Health System hospitals. Covers patient
arrival through disposition (admission, discharge, or transfer).

3. TRIAGE AND WAIT TIME THRESHOLDS

3.1 Triage must be completed within 5 minutes of patient arrival.
3.2 Provider-seen-time thresholds by acuity:

    Acuity Level 1 (Resuscitation): Immediate -- 0 minutes
    Acuity Level 2 (Emergent): 15 minutes maximum
    Acuity Level 3 (Urgent): 30 minutes maximum
    Acuity Level 4 (Less Urgent): 60 minutes maximum
    Acuity Level 5 (Non-Urgent): 120 minutes maximum

3.3 Any wait exceeding the threshold is classified as a "threshold breach" and
    triggers the escalation procedure in Section 5.

4. PATIENT FLOW PROTOCOLS

4.1 Vertical Patient Flow:
    a) Low-acuity patients (4-5) are routed to the fast-track area when available.
    b) Fast-track staffing: minimum 1 provider + 1 nurse during peak hours
       (10 AM - 10 PM).
    c) Target fast-track door-to-disposition: 90 minutes.

4.2 Bed Management:
    a) ED charge nurse monitors bed board every 30 minutes.
    b) When ED census exceeds 80% of licensed beds, the charge nurse activates
       the surge protocol (Section 6).
    c) Admitted patients in ED beds > 2 hours awaiting inpatient bed triggers
       "boarding alert" to house supervisor.

4.3 Diagnostic Turnaround:
    a) Lab results target: 60 minutes from order to result.
    b) Imaging turnaround target: 45 minutes for X-ray, 90 minutes for CT.
    c) Delays beyond targets require notification to ED attending.

5. ESCALATION PROCEDURE

5.1 Level 1 -- Wait Time Breach (any single patient):
    - Charge nurse notified immediately
    - Document reason for delay in EHR
    - Reassign provider resources if possible

5.2 Level 2 -- Multiple Breaches (> 3 patients breaching in 1 hour):
    - ED Medical Director notified
    - Consider opening additional treatment spaces
    - Pull on-call provider if available

5.3 Level 3 -- Sustained Capacity Crisis (> 20% breach rate over 4 hours):
    - Hospital Administrator on Call notified
    - Activate surge protocol (Section 6)
    - Consider ambulance diversion (requires CMO approval)

6. SURGE PROTOCOL

6.1 Trigger: ED census > 80% OR average wait time > 60 minutes for 2+ hours.
6.2 Actions:
    a) Open hallway treatment spaces per pre-approved layout
    b) Activate additional nursing staff (per staffing SOP)
    c) Expedite inpatient discharges -- notify all departments of surge status
    d) Deploy house supervisor to ED for real-time bed management
    e) Activate rapid admission protocol (bypass standard admission order sets
       for stable patients)

6.3 De-escalation: Surge protocol ends when ED census drops below 60% AND
    average wait time < 30 minutes for 1 hour.

7. PERFORMANCE METRICS

7.1 Monthly Dashboard Metrics:
    - Door-to-provider time by acuity (median and 90th percentile)
    - Total threshold breaches by acuity
    - LWBS rate (target: < 2%)
    - ED boarding hours (target: < 4 hours average)
    - Surge protocol activations

7.2 Quarterly Review:
    - Trend analysis of wait times and breach rates
    - Staffing adequacy assessment
    - Patient satisfaction scores (Press Ganey ED section)

8. STAFFING GUIDELINES

8.1 Minimum staffing ratios:
    - 1 provider per 10 ED beds during standard operations
    - 1 nurse per 4 patients for acuity 1-3
    - 1 nurse per 6 patients for acuity 4-5
8.2 Contract labor may supplement during surge but should not exceed 25% of
    total ED nursing hours (see Staffing SOP-003).
8.3 Night shift (10 PM - 6 AM) staffing reduced to 70% of day shift unless
    historical volume warrants full staffing.
