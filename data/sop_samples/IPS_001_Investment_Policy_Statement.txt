INVESTMENT POLICY STATEMENT (IPS)
Document ID: IPS-001
Effective Date: 2024-01-15
Last Review: 2025-06-01
Owner: Chief Investment Officer

1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This Investment Policy Statement establishes the investment objectives, guidelines, and constraints for the Medical Grants Investment Portfolio. It applies to all managed fund allocations, new investments, and portfolio rebalancing activities.

2. INVESTMENT OBJECTIVES

2.1 Primary Objective: Achieve a net-of-fees total return that exceeds the blended benchmark by 150-200 basis points annually over a rolling 5-year period.

2.2 Secondary Objective: Maintain sufficient liquidity to meet annual grant disbursement requirements (estimated at 5-7% of total portfolio value annually).

2.3 Risk Tolerance: Moderate. Maximum drawdown target of 15% in any rolling 12-month period. Portfolio volatility target of 8-12% annualized.

3. ASSET ALLOCATION GUIDELINES

3.1 Strategic Allocation Targets:
- Public Equity: 30-40% (target 35%)
- Private Credit: 20-30% (target 25%)
- Venture Capital: 10-20% (target 15%)
- Real Assets: 5-15% (target 10%)
- Hedge Funds: 5-15% (target 10%)
- Cash/Short-term: 2-5% (target 5%)

3.2 Rebalancing Policy: Rebalance when any asset class deviates more than 5 percentage points from its target allocation. Quarterly review required.

3.3 Concentration Limits:
- No single fund to exceed 10% of total portfolio
- No single manager to exceed 15% of total portfolio
- Top-5 fund concentration must not exceed 40% of any strategy segment

4. MANAGER SELECTION CRITERIA

4.1 Minimum Qualifications:
- Track record of 3+ years with audited returns
- AUM of at least $100M
- Registered with appropriate regulatory bodies
- Annual independent audit requirement

4.2 Evaluation Factors:
- Risk-adjusted returns (Sharpe ratio > 0.8 preferred)
- Consistency of returns across market cycles
- Team stability and key-person risk assessment
- Operational due diligence (ODD) pass required
- ESG integration capabilities

5. LIQUIDITY MANAGEMENT

5.1 Liquidity Reserve: Maintain minimum 12 months of projected grant disbursements in liquid assets (Monthly or Quarterly redemption terms).

5.2 Illiquidity Budget: No more than 35% of total portfolio in locked or annual-redemption vehicles.

5.3 Capital Call Management: Maintain unfunded commitment reserve of at least 110% of projected 12-month capital calls.

6. RISK MANAGEMENT

6.1 Monitoring Frequency: Weekly NAV monitoring, monthly performance attribution, quarterly risk assessment.

6.2 Watchlist Criteria: Place fund on watchlist if:
- Returns underperform benchmark by >300bps for 2 consecutive quarters
- Key-person departure
- Regulatory action or compliance issue
- AUM decline >20% from peak
- Style drift detected

6.3 Redemption Triggers: Initiate redemption review if:
- Fund on watchlist for 2+ consecutive quarters
- Breach of investment guidelines communicated by manager
- Material change in fund strategy or terms

7. REPORTING REQUIREMENTS

7.1 Monthly: NAV, returns, and cash flow summary for all funds
7.2 Quarterly: Full portfolio review including attribution, risk metrics, and compliance check
7.3 Annually: Comprehensive IPS review, manager evaluation, and strategic allocation assessment

8. GOVERNANCE

8.1 Investment Committee: Reviews and approves all new investments >$10M, strategy changes, and IPS amendments.
8.2 CIO Authority: May execute investments <$10M within approved guidelines without Committee approval.
8.3 Compliance Officer: Monitors adherence to IPS guidelines and reports violations to Investment Committee.
