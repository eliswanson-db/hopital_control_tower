PORTFOLIO ALLOCATION AND REBALANCING POLICY
Document ID: IPS-003
Effective Date: 2024-02-01
Last Review: 2025-06-01
Owner: Portfolio Manager

1. PURPOSE

This document establishes procedures for portfolio construction, allocation decisions, and rebalancing activities in alignment with the Investment Policy Statement (IPS-001).

2. STRATEGIC ASSET ALLOCATION

2.1 Target Portfolio:
- Public Equity: 35% (range: 30-40%)
- Private Credit: 25% (range: 20-30%)
- Venture Capital: 15% (range: 10-20%)
- Real Assets: 10% (range: 5-15%)
- Hedge Funds: 10% (range: 5-15%)
- Cash Reserve: 5% (range: 2-5%)

2.2 Benchmark Composition:
- Public Equity: MSCI World Healthcare Index
- Private Credit: Cliffwater Direct Lending Index
- Venture Capital: Cambridge Associates US Venture Capital Index
- Real Assets: NCREIF Property Index + S&P Global Infrastructure
- Hedge Funds: HFRI Fund Weighted Composite Index

3. REBALANCING PROCEDURES

3.1 Trigger-Based Rebalancing:
- Hard trigger: Any asset class deviates >5% from target -> immediate rebalancing required
- Soft trigger: Any asset class deviates >3% from target -> flag for next quarterly review
- Cash reserve below 2% -> immediate liquidity action required

3.2 Calendar-Based Review:
- Quarterly: Full allocation review vs targets
- Semi-annually: Strategic allocation review with forward-looking adjustments
- Annually: Comprehensive IPS and allocation methodology review

3.3 Rebalancing Execution:
Step 1: Identify overweight and underweight positions
Step 2: Assess transaction costs and tax implications
Step 3: Prioritize liquid redemptions for overweight reduction
Step 4: Deploy to underweight strategies via approved fund pipeline
Step 5: Document rationale and approval in rebalancing log

4. NEW ALLOCATION PROCEDURES

4.1 Pipeline Management:
- Maintain pipeline of 10-15 pre-qualified funds per strategy
- Quarterly pipeline review and refresh
- New funds require full due diligence (per IPS-002) before allocation

4.2 Sizing Guidelines:
- Initial allocation: 2-5% of total portfolio per fund
- Maximum single fund: 10% of total portfolio
- Maximum single manager: 15% across all funds
- Vintage year diversification: spread commitments across 3+ vintages

4.3 Approval Thresholds:
- <$5M: CIO approval
- $5M-$10M: CIO + one Investment Committee member
- >$10M: Full Investment Committee approval

5. CONCENTRATION MONITORING

5.1 Fund-Level:
- Alert at 8% of portfolio in single fund
- Hard limit at 10% -- must initiate reduction plan

5.2 Manager-Level:
- Alert at 12% of portfolio with single manager
- Hard limit at 15% -- must diversify

5.3 Strategy-Level:
- Top-5 fund concentration within any strategy must not exceed 40%
- If breached, initiate diversification within 2 quarters

5.4 Sector Concentration:
- Healthcare sector: permitted up to 60% given portfolio mandate
- Any other single sector: maximum 25%
- Geographic concentration: no single geography >50% (except North America)

6. LIQUIDITY MANAGEMENT

6.1 Liquidity Tiers:
- Tier 1 (Daily/Weekly): Cash, money market -> minimum 2% of portfolio
- Tier 2 (Monthly): Liquid hedge funds, public equity -> minimum 20%
- Tier 3 (Quarterly): Credit funds, some hedge funds -> up to 30%
- Tier 4 (Annual+): Private equity, venture, locked vehicles -> maximum 35%

6.2 Cash Flow Forecasting:
- Monthly: Project capital calls and distributions for next 12 months
- Quarterly: Update grant disbursement forecast
- Annually: 3-year liquidity stress test

7. DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS

All allocation decisions must include:
- Investment memo with rationale
- Fit within strategic allocation targets
- Impact on concentration limits
- Liquidity assessment
- Approval signatures per threshold
