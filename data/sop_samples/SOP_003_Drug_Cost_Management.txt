MEMORIAL HEALTH SYSTEM -- STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
Document: SOP-003-DC
Title: Drug Cost Management and Formulary Compliance
Effective Date: 2024-02-01
Review Date: 2025-02-01
Department: Pharmacy / All Clinical Departments
Approved By: Chief Pharmacy Officer, Chief Financial Officer

1. PURPOSE

This procedure establishes protocols for managing pharmaceutical costs, ensuring
formulary compliance, investigating cost anomalies, and optimizing drug utilization
across all Memorial Health System facilities.

2. SCOPE

Applies to all drug orders, dispensing, and procurement activities across Hospital A,
Hospital B, and Hospital C. Covers inpatient, ED, and outpatient pharmacy operations.

3. FORMULARY MANAGEMENT

3.1 The Pharmacy and Therapeutics (P&T) Committee maintains the system formulary.
3.2 P&T Committee reviews formulary quarterly. Criteria for inclusion:
    - Clinical efficacy evidence
    - Cost-effectiveness relative to therapeutic alternatives
    - Safety profile
    - Availability and supply chain reliability
3.3 Non-formulary drug requests require attending physician justification and
    pharmacy director approval. Target: < 5% of orders are non-formulary.
3.4 Therapeutic interchange: Pharmacists may substitute formulary equivalents per
    the approved interchange list without additional physician authorization.

4. COST MONITORING AND THRESHOLDS

4.1 Drug cost per encounter is tracked daily by hospital and department.
4.2 Baseline: System-wide drug cost per encounter is established quarterly.
4.3 Alert Thresholds:
    a) Department drug cost > 20% above quarterly baseline: Yellow alert --
       pharmacy director reviews within 5 business days.
    b) Department drug cost > 40% above quarterly baseline: Red alert --
       investigation initiated within 48 hours (see Section 5).
    c) Single drug category cost increase > 30% month-over-month: Automatic
       P&T Committee review.

4.4 Monthly reporting includes:
    - Total pharmacy spend by hospital, department, and drug category
    - Cost per encounter trends (6-month rolling)
    - Top 10 drugs by total spend
    - Non-formulary utilization rate
    - Therapeutic category breakdown

5. COST SPIKE INVESTIGATION PROCEDURE

5.1 Trigger: Any red alert (Section 4.3b) or unexplained cost increase > 25%
    month-over-month.

5.2 Investigation Steps:
    Step 1: Pharmacy analyst pulls 30-day cost data for the affected
            department and drug categories.
    Step 2: Compare unit cost vs. quantity changes to determine whether the
            spike is price-driven or volume-driven.
    Step 3: If price-driven:
            - Check for contract changes or GPO pricing updates
            - Verify no procurement errors or duplicate orders
            - Escalate to supply chain if vendor pricing changed
    Step 4: If volume-driven:
            - Correlate with patient volume changes (census, acuity mix)
            - Check for new clinical protocols that increased utilization
            - Identify specific physicians or departments driving volume
    Step 5: Document findings and corrective actions in the cost management log.
    Step 6: Present findings at next P&T Committee meeting.

5.3 Timeline: Investigation must be completed within 10 business days of trigger.

6. HIGH-COST DRUG MANAGEMENT

6.1 Drugs costing > $500 per dose require attending physician order AND
    pharmacist verification of clinical appropriateness.
6.2 Specialty drugs (biologics, oncology agents) require prior authorization
    through the specialty pharmacy program.
6.3 Monthly high-cost drug utilization report provided to department medical
    directors showing:
    - Patient count and total cost per high-cost drug
    - Comparison to expected utilization based on patient mix
    - Waste tracking (unused doses, expired inventory)

7. DEPARTMENT-SPECIFIC GUIDELINES

7.1 Cardiology: Primary cost drivers are anticoagulants and antiarrhythmics.
    Generic substitution rate target: > 85%. Monthly review of heparin and
    DOAC utilization by pharmacy clinical specialist.

7.2 Oncology: Drug costs managed through a separate oncology drug budget.
    Biosimilar adoption target: > 60% for available biosimilar agents.
    Regimen standardization tracked against NCCN guidelines.

7.3 Emergency Department: Formulary compliance is critical for cost control.
    ED-specific formulary subset maintained. Contract medication pricing
    reviewed quarterly with ED medical director.

7.4 Orthopedics: Surgical implant costs tracked separately. Antibiotic
    prophylaxis standardized per SCIP guidelines to reduce variation.

8. VENDOR AND SUPPLY CHAIN

8.1 GPO (Group Purchasing Organization) contract compliance reviewed monthly.
    Target: > 95% compliance with GPO contracts.
8.2 340B program compliance monitored for eligible facilities.
8.3 Drug shortage management: pharmacy maintains a 30-day buffer stock for
    critical medications. Shortage notifications distributed within 24 hours.

9. PERFORMANCE METRICS

9.1 Dashboard Metrics (updated monthly):
    - Drug cost per encounter by hospital and department
    - Formulary compliance rate
    - Non-formulary utilization rate
    - Generic substitution rate
    - Cost per DRG vs. benchmark
    - Year-over-year cost trend

9.2 Annual Goals:
    - Drug cost per encounter growth: < inflation + 2%
    - Formulary compliance: > 95%
    - Generic substitution: > 80%
    - Investigation close rate: 100% within 10 business days
